USE OF ALCOHOL ISOPROPIL

In the early use of isopropyl alcohol can be technically used for anesthesia, many negative deficiencies prohibit this use. Isopropyl alcohol may also be used similarly to the ether as a solvent [20] or as an anesthetic by inhaling smoke or orally. Initial uses include using solvents as general anesthesia for small mammals [21] and mice by scientists and some veterinarians. However, it is immediately stopped, as many complications arise, including respiratory irritation, internal bleeding, and visual and auditory problems. In rare cases, respiratory failure causes death in animals observed. In 1990, 45 thousand tons of isopropyl alcohol was used in the US In that year, 5.4 tons were consumed for domestic use and in personal care products. Isopropyl alcohol is particularly popular for pharmaceutical applications, [11] it is thought to be due to the low toxicity of any residue. Some isopropyl alcohols are used as chemical intermediates. Isopropyl alcohol can be converted to acetone, but the cumene process is more significant. It is also used as a gasoline additive. Isopropyl alcohol dissolves a variety of non-polar compounds. Therefore, it is widely used as a solvent and as a cleaning fluid, especially for dissolving oil. Together with ethanol, n-butanol and methanol, it belongs to the alcohol solvent group, approximately 6.4 million tonnes are used worldwide in 2011. Examples of these applications include electronic cleaning of devices such as contact pins (such as on ROM cartridges) , magnetic tape and disk head (as in audio and video tape recorders and floppy disk drives), laser lenses on optical disc drives (eg CDs, DVDs) and removes thermal paste from heatsinks and pack ICs (such as CPUs).
